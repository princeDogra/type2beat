According to Diabetes Australia, for senior citizens, there are basically two types of diabetes, including type 1 diabetes, type 2 diabetes.


Symptoms

  • For type 1 diabetes, the symptoms may include:
    • Excessive thirst
    • Frequent urination
    • Excessive hunger
    • Unexplained weakness and fatigue
    • Unexplained weight loss
    • Blurred vision
    • Having cuts that heal slowly
    • Itching and skin infections
    • Mood swings
    • Headache
    • Dizziness
    • Leg cramps
  • For type 2 diabetes, the symptoms may include:
    • Blurred vision
    • Tiredness
    • Urinating more frequently
    • Feeling thirsty all the time
    • Numbness and tingling in the feet or legs
    • Recurrent infections
    • Always feeling hungry
    • Having cuts that heal slowly
    • Itching, skin infections
    • Gradually putting on weight
    • Mood swings
    • Headaches
    • Feeling dizzy
    • Leg cramps

Causes:

  • The cause of type 1 diabetes may include:
    • Viral or bacterial infection
    • Chemical toxins within food
    • Unidentified component causing autoimmune reaction
    • Underlying genetic disposition
  • The cause of type 2 diabetes may include:
    • Obesity
    • Living a sedentary lifestyle
    • Increasing age
    • Bad diet

Consequences:

    • Never ending demands of diabetes care
    • Eating carefully
    • Monitoring blood glucose
    • Scheduling and planning
    • Impact on your driving
    • Impact on your working

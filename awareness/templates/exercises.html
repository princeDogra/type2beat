Exercises for Preventing Diabetes

  1. Home

Exercise helps reduce the risk of having diabetes, for those who have diabetes, it is also an important role in keeping you healthy.

What Are the Benefits of Exercise?

  • Exercise maintains your body weight at a healthy level.
  • Blood pressure can be lower by doing exercise.
  • Be more energetic to reduce your stress.
  • It helps insulin levels to improve your diabetes management.

    • Exercise Programs:

    It is important to see your doctor before doing any exercise that has potential risks. Start with something easy and fun, you won’t be doing it on the next day if you don’t like it.


    What Exercise Can You do?

    Walking


    Walking is a great solution to help you manage your diabetes, it helps you maintain a steady blood glucose level and body weight. It is recommended to have a 30 minutes walk at least 5 days a week.

    Swimming


    Many senior citizens are suffering from joint disease, while swimming would be the best exercise for them. When swimming, your body muscles will be stretched and relaxed, and it doesn’t put extra pressure on your joints, it can not only steady your blood glucose levels but also improve your stress.

    Cycling


    Cycling would be another solution for improving your health, it is indicated that cycling at a moderate pace for an hour can greatly reduce their blood glucose levels to half of it in the next 24 hours. But it is also important to keep your safety when cycling, try to control your pace and cycle on level ground or with only a few hills.

    Gradening


    Gardening is for everyone, especially for those with diabetes. It is indicated that the lack of vitamin D could increase the risk of type 2 diabetes, while gardening provides you with more time enjoying the natural sunlight. Gardening is often more time consuming compared with other exercises, which has a great effect on lowering your blood glucose levels. With gardening, you decide what you see and what you eat, it can both relieve your stress and make your diet more healthy.

    Taichi


    Tai Chi is A Chinese martial art that has been practiced for generations. A study showed that gentle exercise can prevent diabetes in 60% of cases. It focuses on your breath and mind. There is also a specially designed program supported by Diabetes Australia called ‘Tai Chi for Diabetes’.

    Water Aerobics

    Water Aerobics is like other exercises, which helps manage your diabetes and lower blood glucose levels. It can also contribute to your body composition in a positive way, which improves your overall quality of life.

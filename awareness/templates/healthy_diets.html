Food is important if you want to control your diabetes, learning about a healthy diet can also benefit everyone. There are many diet plans, we are here to recommend some plans that could effectively reduce your blood glucose levels or maintain it at a normal level.

Which Diet Is Right For Me?


{% endblock header %} {% block main %}

    Diet for Type 1 Diabetes

For type 1 diabetes, the advice is not very different from people who don’t have diabetes. First thing is that it is vital to count your carbohydrate intake, as it can balance your insulin intake with what you eat and drink. It is recommended to have a balanced diet, containing a variety of different vegetables, natural food is the best choice compared with other processed foods.

    Diet for Type 2 Diabetes

For type 2 diabetes, it is important to understand how sugar in the food will affect your blood glucose levels, generally speaking, your diet should contain a variety of different vegetables, which can provide you with different nutrition your body needs, unsaturated fats sources, such as nuts and avocados. Also, it is vital that you avoid processed foods. It is also reported that low-carb diets can improve blood glucose levels.

    Vegan Diet

The idea of a vegan diet has been changing, generally, it means cutting out meat, dairy products. Although the foods you eat are limited, it can definitely improve your blood glucose levels. First, a vegan diet is low in cholesterol and saturated fat, but your body can still get enough fiber and protein, that’s why your blood glucose can be improved.

    Vegetarian Diet

Different from the vegan diet, the vegetarian diet includes dairy products, like cheese and milk, it can also contain other products derived from an animal. But the principle remains the same as the vegan diet.

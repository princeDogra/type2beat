Diet for Type 1 Diabetes

The dietary recommendations usually provided for patients with type 1 diabetes are not much different from the dietary recommendations for patients without diabetes. The main question to consider is how much different foods may affect blood sugar levels, and how to balance carbohydrates with the right amount of insulin.


A balanced diet can help prevent the development of diabetes. A balanced diet, which contains a variety of different vegetables, helps the body absorb different nutrients. In addition, we strongly recommend that the diet should contain nuts, avocados and fish. Such foods containing unsaturated fatty acids can help adjust various functions of the body.


Finally, we recommend limiting the amount of processed food you eat and including home-made or freshly cooked food whenever possible.

Diet for Type 2 Diabetes

The diet for type 2 diabetes should follow the principles of a healthy diet, with the focus on avoiding foods that adversely affect blood glucose levels. As a general guideline, the diet should include sources of unsaturated food such as various vegetables, nuts, avocados, and oily fish, while avoiding processed foods.


Type 2 Diabetes and NHS Diet

According to the NHS's dietary recommendations, you should eat starchy carbohydrates, as well as more fruits and vegetables, and less saturated fat, salt, and sugar at every meal. But many people with type 2 diabetes find that even amyloid carbohydrates with low GI content tend to significantly increase blood sugar levels.


Low-Crab Diest and Type 2 Diabetes

Low-carb diets are becoming more common in patients with type 2 diabetes. Many diabetics report that blood sugar levels have improved in low-carb diets, which also helps reduce the substitution of drugs.

Vegan Diet

Many people are immediately afraid of vegan food, but this attitude is gradually changing, especially among diabetics. Can diabetics use a vegan diet to improve blood sugar control? Absolutely, yes!


Diabetics can control blood glucose levels more easily by eating a healthy vegan diet that is low in cholesterol and saturated fat. But a healthy vegetarian diet should be balanced enough to include fibre and protein.


This type of diet can help to limit glucose levels and provide better control of diabetes, especially when combined with exercise.

Vegetarian Diet

Diet is important for maintaining the health of all people. Particularly for diabetics, choosing a sustainable and healthy diet is very significant.


Diet is one of the most important ways to control diabetes. Combining appropriate exercise and medical treatment can provide an effective way to maintain the stability of glucose level.


A well-balanced vegetarian diet, with an emphasis on low fat, high fibres, and high carbohydrates can be particularly suitable for diabetic patients.

    Eat Health Food

Learning about food is one of the best ways to control type 2 diabetes, but eating a healthy diet can benefit all people with diabetes.

Eating the right diet for your individual condition means learning about different foods, how they affect your body, food preparation, when and how much of it you should eat, and what it will do to your blood glucose levels.

Learn More

    Drink Enough Water

As water contains no carbohydrate or calories, it is the perfect drink for people with diabetes. Studies have also shown that drinking water could help control blood glucose levels.

In addition, the high blood sugar levels can cause dehydration for the diabetics. Thus, drinking enough water can help your body eliminate excess glucose through urine.

Learn More

    Exerise Regularly

People with diabetes are recommended to exercise regularly for better blood sugar control and to reduce the risk of cardiovascular diseases. The reason for this is that muscles which are working use more glucose than those that are resting. Muscle movement leads to greater sugar uptake by muscle cells and lower blood sugar levels. In addition, exercise regularly can bring lots of benefit for diabetes include a healthier heart, better weight control and stress management.

Learn More

    Avoid Fasting

Fasting may not be part of your daily work, but this is something that many people do occasionally. For example, believers in Buddhism and Islam need to observe fasting. Or in some operations involving anesthesia, fasting is also necessary.

But if you have diabetes, fasting requires a special and professional plan. That's because you don't want to risk taking too much blood sugar, which will seriously affect your health. We will refer to the recommendations of professional health personnel to provide patients with fasting guidelines.
